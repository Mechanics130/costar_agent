# CoStar Pitch

## 100-word version

CoStar is an open-core skill engine for durable relationship context. It turns
messy notes, meetings, and transcripts into confirmed people profiles, meeting
briefings, roleplay simulations, graph views, and persistent markdown views.
Instead of asking users to rebuild context by hand, CoStar keeps relationship
memory alive across time. It is designed for developers and product builders
who want a self-hostable relationship intelligence layer today, and for a
future consumer product that gives people a better chief-of-staff experience.

## 300-word version

CoStar is an open-core skill engine for durable relationship context. It helps
people and teams turn messy notes, meeting minutes, transcripts, and historical
materials into something that can actually be used later: confirmed people
profiles, relationship views, briefing notes, graph summaries, and simulated
conversation prep.

The core idea is simple. Most tools let you store information. CoStar helps you
extract the people inside the information, confirm the important updates, and
keep those updates alive over time. That means the next meeting starts with more
context, less rework, and a better chance of making the right move.

CoStar is open-core because the engine itself is valuable to engineers,
founders, and product builders who want control, privacy, and custom workflows.
The consumer experience can sit on top of it as a separate hosted product. That
lets the platform serve two audiences without mixing their needs:

- the open-core skill engine for builders
- the consumer chief-of-staff product for end users

If you need a relationship intelligence layer that can be self-hosted, wired
into your own model provider, and extended over time, CoStar is the engine
layer to start with.

## 500-word version

CoStar is an open-core skill engine for durable relationship context. It is
designed to take raw, messy, and high-volume human material - notes, meetings,
transcripts, profile snippets, and historical records - and convert it into a
usable memory layer for people-focused work.

The engine is built around a small but powerful loop:

1. capture the material
2. identify the people inside it
3. confirm the important updates
4. write the changes back into durable profiles
5. keep those profiles alive through views, briefs, roleplay, and graph outputs

That matters because most teams lose context between meetings. They remember
the facts that happened recently, but not the subtle preferences, boundaries,
intentions, and relationship dynamics that make the next conversation work.
CoStar helps preserve those signals so that the next action is based on the
latest confirmed context, not a stale memory.

CoStar is intentionally open-core. The repo gives builders the skill engine:
profile ingestion, review resolution, briefing, roleplay, graphing, and
persistent views. That makes it useful to developers, product managers, and
teams who want to own their own context stack. A separate hosted consumer layer
can sit on top of this engine for non-technical users.

That split is important. Builders get control and extensibility. End users get a
simple chief-of-staff-style experience that feels reliable without exposing the
engine details.

If you are trying to build a relationship CRM, a personal chief of staff, a
people-memory layer, or a self-hostable context engine, CoStar gives you the
core that keeps the system grounded in durable people context.
